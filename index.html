KOLHAPUR

Perfect city for foodies!

A south-western city in Maharashtra, Kolhapur is massively famous for temple of Mahalaxmi, tranquilizing Rankala lake and Panhala fort for its beauty. The city is particularly known for the Kolhapuri chappal, a hand-crafted buffalo leather slipper that is locally tanned using vegetable dyes.Other handicrafts include hand block printing of textiles, silver, bead and crafting; pottery; wood carving and lacquerware and embroidery making.

Having said all this, Kolhapuri food remains a less discussed topic. It maybe surprising to know that there's a lot more than Kolhapuri tambda and pandhra rassa on the menu. So if you're a foodie and happen to be around Kolhapur, this page is just for you!

Right food for right mood!

Kolhapuris believe in starting their day with a heavy breakfast. Indigenous misal (a popular spicy dish made up of easily-available and nutritional ingridients), Khandoli (a special variety of egg omlette served with bread and onion rings) are must-haves. Along with these native dishes, south-Indian dishes, north-Indian dishes are sure to satisfy your taste buds.Poha, Khichadi and Upma are easily available too.

When it comes to having a meal, a lot of varieties in vegetarian and non-vegetarian food is available. Mutton, chicken and seafood is available in a lot of variety and you are sure not to get disappointed. I have included LESS KNOWN places, which if recognised are sure to please you. I hope you leave with a happy tummy when you leave Kolhapur!

Jhunka Bhaakar

It consists of Roti made up of Jowar complemented with Jhunka or Pitla . It is a semi solid paste made up of gram flour, sautted in oil. Chillies are added to make it hot and spicy. It is usually served with Kharda, which is pickle like add-on made up of tamarind, chillies and nuts.

Mutton Thali

If you're exploring the city and haven't tasted the Mutton Thali yet, you're just not doing it right! The Thalis, rich in flavour and aroma, consist of Rotis, Mutton gravy, a bowl each of Red and white soup, consisting of Mutton pieces with unique Kolhapuri spices, rice and salad. The red soup is famous for its hot taste and high nutrutional value. The white soup consists of coconut milk as the main ingridient that balances the hotness of red soup.

  • Hotel Opal.
